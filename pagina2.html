Sus Logros

Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla (1753–1811) was a Mexican priest and the main leader who started the Mexican War of Independence. He is known in Mexico as the “Father of the Nation.”

Who was he?

Miguel Hidalgo was a smart and educated priest. He cared about poor people and wanted Mexico to be free from Spanish rule.

Important Facts

  • Born: May 8, 1753, in Guanajuato, Mexico

  • Died: July 30, 1811, in Chihuahua, Mexico

  • Job: Priest and leader of the independence movement

What did he do?

  • On September 16, 1810, he gave the famous “Grito de Dolores” (Cry of Dolores), which called the people to fight for independence.

  • He led a large group of farmers, mestizos, and Indigenous people.

  • He wanted to end slavery and unfair taxes.

Major Events

  • He helped lead the attack on the Alhóndiga de Granaditas.

  • He made a law to end slavery in the areas he controlled.

How did he die?

He was captured by Spanish forces, put on trial, and executed on July 30, 1811.

His Legacy

Miguel Hidalgo is remembered as a national hero. Every year, Mexicans celebrate Independence Day with the Grito in his honor.
